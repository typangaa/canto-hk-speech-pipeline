## 3. AUDIO QUALITY ASSESSMENT FOR TTS TRAINING

### 3.1 Twitch VOD Audio Characteristics
- **Format:** Twitch streams are encoded in Opus at 96kbps (standard) or 128kbps (Turbo/Partner)
- **Extraction:** Can be downloaded via twitch-dl, yt-dlp, or Twitch API. Audio extraction via ffmpeg
  yields clean 48kHz Opus audio.
- **Speech Clarity:** Depends on streamer's setup. Partner/Affiliate streamers typically use:
  - USB microphones (Blue Yeti, HyperX QuadCast, etc.) -- good quality
  - Some use XLR setups -- professional quality
  - Quality varies significantly between streamers
- **Noise Issues:**
  - Gaming streams: Background music (BGM), game sound effects, Discord call noise
  - Just Chatting: Cleanest -- single speaker, minimal BG noise
  - Panel/IntStream: Multiple speakers, chat audio, occasional BGM
- **Key Finding:** Just Chatting category is the GOLD STANDARD for TTS. Single speaker, natural
  conversation, no game audio interference, consistent mic quality.

### 3.2 YouTube Content Audio Characteristics
- **Format:** YouTube encodes at AAC 128-192kbps (standard) or up to 256kbps (premium)
- **Extraction:** yt-dlp can extract 192kbps AAC audio from YouTube videos
- **Speech Clarity:** Generally excellent -- YouTube content creators use professional setups.
  Cooking shows and podcasts have the cleanest audio.
- **Noise Issues:** Minimal for most content. Cooking shows are nearly pristine.
  Vlogs may have ambient noise. Comedy sketches may have intentional sound effects.

### 3.3 Recommended Processing Pipeline
For TTS training, the following pipeline is recommended:
1. **Download:** Use yt-dlp for YouTube, twitch-dl or YouTube DLG for Twitch VODs
2. **Audio Extraction:** Convert to WAV/FLAC (48kHz, 16-bit) for maximum quality
3. **Voice Activity Detection (VAD):** Use Silero VAD to isolate speech segments
4. **Noise Reduction:** Apply RNNoise or similar AI noise suppression
5. **Diarization:** Use Pyannote or similar to separate speakers in multi-speaker content
6. **Segmentation:** Split into 2-10 second segments for TTS training
7. **Quality Filter:** Remove segments with low SNR, excessive noise, or overlapping speech

## 5. KEY CONSTRAINTS & CONSIDERATIONS

### 5.1 Twitch VOD Retention Policy (Critical!)
- Free users: VODs deleted after 7 days
- Affiliate: VODs deleted after 14 days
- Partner/Turbo: VODs deleted after 60 days
- **100-hour storage limit for Highlights & Uploads** (implemented April 2025)
- **Action Required:** Must archive VODs quickly before they expire. Use tools like
  StreamRecorder, Eklipse, or manual yt-dlp downloading.

### 5.2 Copyright Considerations
- Most Twitch VODs are under Creator's copyright. Personal/educational use likely covered by
  fair use for TTS corpus, but commercial use would need permission.
- YouTube content: Generally more permissive for archival purposes.
- Best practice: Contact streamers for permission, especially for public dataset distribution.

### 5.3 Speaker Diversity
- Current list heavily skews female (艾怡, 雅麗, 清兒). Need more male speakers.
- VTubers may have pitch-shifted voices -- verify before using for TTS.
- Cooking content (馬田) = single speaker, great quality but limited speaker diversity.

### 5.4 Language Code
- Twitch uses language tag: `Cantonese` (for filtering)
- YouTube uses language tag: `zh-HK` (for filtering)
